Population Study

For this project I analyzed the population growth/decline of various animal species in their natural habitats. By analyzing statistical data for a specific animal species I could determine, based on the current size of a population and the statistical data, if a species population will grow or if it will shrink. If the population is growing I then determined to what extent, grow seemingly unbounded or stabilize at a certain size. Also if a population is growing and is one that may be hunted I then caclulated the percentage of the population that may be harvested in a given season without risking running the population to extinction. On the other hand if a population is shrinking, determine if it will stabilize or go extinct if not aided. The software I used to analyze and model this problem was matlab.

Optimized College Diet

Using methods of linear optimization I did exactly what the title of this problem suggest. Following the USDA suggestions of nutritional requirements I determined the cheapest possible diet to satisfy these requirements. This meant finding a diet that will provide one with all the necessary vitamins, minerals for a day while consming between two and three thousand calories a day. I determined that one could satisfy all needs on a $1.59 a day or roughly 45 dollars a month. The solution to my original model was feasible but lacked some constraints, 45 dollars a month sounds good but this meant consuming over 2 pounds of pinto beans a day, while feasible it is highly unlikely for one day let alone repetively done as a diet. After solving my original model I made two modications to it, adding constraints on what I am willing/wanting to eat and constraints to add day to day variety.
